## Core USP

Flores Clean specializes in commercial spaces — not homes. Every crew member is background-checked and bonded. They offer guaranteed same-day response for urgent cleaning needs and customizable recurring schedules (daily, weekly, bi-weekly). Their edge: a dedicated account manager for every client, so you never explain your needs twice.

## Site Archetype

**Modern Specialist** — Confident, technical, focused. Commercial cleaning is a niche within cleaning; Flores Clean positions itself as the expert partner for businesses, not a generalist maid service.

## Signature Moves

1. **Dark, editorial homepage layout** — Instead of a bright, friendly cleaning site, use a sophisticated dark theme with high-contrast typography and sharp photography overlays. This signals "corporate partner" not "cleaning lady."
2. **Client type selector** — A visual module on the homepage where visitors click their facility type (office, medical, retail, construction site) and see tailored messaging/services for that vertical. Not just a services grid.

## Brand Direction

- **Tone:** Professional, confident, modern — not bubbly, not domestic. Speaks business-to-business.
- **Feel:** Like hiring a facilities management partner, not a cleaning crew. Sleek, organized, precise.
- **Theme:** NO preset DaisyUI theme. Fully custom dark palette.
- **Colors:** Deep charcoal/near-black base (#0a0a0a, #141414), cool slate greys (#2a2e35, #4a5568), electric teal accent (#0ea5a9) for CTAs and highlights, crisp white (#f5f5f5) for text. Dark and premium, not gloomy.
- **Typography:** Sharp, modern sans-serif for headings (something geometric and confident — Urbanist or Sora), clean readable body font (Outfit or Manrope). Type should feel precise, not decorative.
- **Textures:** Subtle geometric patterns, clean lines, slight gradient overlays on dark surfaces. No organic textures. Think: glass office building, not cozy home.
